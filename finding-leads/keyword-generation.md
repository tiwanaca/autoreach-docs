# Keyword Generation

Let AI generate targeted search queries tailored to your offer. AutoReach creates platform-optimized keywords that power recurring daily searches and discovery across X and LinkedIn.

## How It Works

### AI-Powered Query Generation

When you create an offer or start a search, AutoReach:
1. Analyzes your offer description, pain points, and target audience
2. Generates 10-20 focused search queries per platform
3. Adapts format to each platform's native search syntax
4. Derives keywords from your specific offer domain
5. Weights niche terminology and industry jargon heavily

This ensures keywords are tailored to your exact solution, not generic terms.

## X/Twitter Keyword Generation

Twitter keywords use native search operators for precision:

### Supported Operators
- **AND:** Combine terms (e.g., "sales" AND "automation")
- **OR:** Alternative terms (e.g., "CRM" OR "Salesforce")
- **-exclude:** Remove false positives (e.g., -crypto, -NFT)

### Generated Query Examples

For a B2B Sales Automation Platform:
- `"sales process" AND "manual" -NFT`
- `"pipeline management" OR "forecast accuracy"`
- `"sales operations" AND "struggling"`
- `"CRM automation" -crypto`
- `"sales team efficiency" AND "bottleneck"`

For a People Analytics Platform:
- `"employee retention" AND "trends"`
- `"talent analytics" OR "workforce insights"`
- `"people operations" AND "visibility"`
- `"HR metrics" AND "struggling"`
- `"team analytics" -football`

### Specificity Scoring

AutoReach prefers multi-word phrases:

**Higher specificity (preferred):**
- ✓ "sales pipeline visibility" (3 words, specific)
- ✓ "manual sales process" (3 words, concrete problem)
- ✓ "forecasting accuracy" (2 words, domain-specific)

**Lower specificity (deprioritized):**
- ✗ "sales" (1 word, generic)
- ✗ "CRM" (1 word, too broad)
- ✗ "automation" (1 word, vague)

Multi-word queries reduce noise and find higher-intent prospects.

## Keyword Sourcing

Keywords are intelligently derived from multiple elements of your offer:

### Primary Sources
1. **Pain points** from your offer description
2. **Target audience description** (titles, industries)
3. **Search signals** you've provided (natural language phrases)
4. **Competitor keywords** (tools/solutions in your space)

### Secondary Sources
- Your offer's unique terminology
- Industry jargon and vertical-specific language
- Common problem statements in your market
- Known decision-maker conversation patterns

### Explicit Keyword Input

You can also manually specify keywords you know are relevant:
- Override or supplement AI generation
- Provide high-confidence search terms you've validated
- Add niche terminology the AI might miss

## Platform-Specific Optimization

### Twitter Optimization
- Shorter phrases (2-3 words optimal)
- More specific jargon acceptable
- Operator syntax native to Twitter search
- Intent-oriented language ("struggling", "frustrated", "need")

### LinkedIn Optimization
- Slightly longer phrases (2-4 words)
- Professional terminology
- Role/title-inclusive language
- Solution-seeking language ("looking for", "evaluating", "implement")

## Best Practices

1. **Review AI-generated keywords:** AutoReach generates good keywords, but you know your market. Review and customize if needed.

2. **Include industry jargon:** Technical terminology specific to your vertical generates higher-quality prospects.

3. **Vary intent signals:** Use a mix of pain-focused ("struggling with"), growth-focused ("scaling"), and evaluation-focused ("comparing") keywords.

4. **Test and iterate:** If certain keywords generate low-quality prospects, disable them and try variations.

5. **Monitor recurring searches:** Check the quality and volume of prospects from daily searches. Adjust keywords if needed.

6. **Update keywords with offer changes:** When you refine your offer description, regenerate keywords to stay aligned.

7. **Combine with other discovery:** Keywords should complement content search, people search, and lookalike audiences, not replace them.

## Keyword Quality Metrics

Monitor these metrics to evaluate keyword effectiveness:

| Metric | Target | Action if Low |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| **ICP Match Rate** | 20-40% | Keywords too broad, refine pain points |
| **Prospect Volume** | 50-200 per day | Keywords too specific, broaden slightly |
| **Response Rate** | 5-15% | Quality good, use for recurring searches |
| **Duplicate Rate** | 10-20% | Normal, same prospect posts multiple times |

## Troubleshooting

**Getting too many irrelevant results?**
- Keywords may be too generic
- Review AI-generated keywords and make them more specific
- Add exclusion terms for common false positives
- Include more niche/vertical-specific terminology

**Not getting enough results?**
- Keywords may be too specific/niche
- Broaden slightly while maintaining specificity
- Add synonym variations
- Check if your target audience actually discusses these topics

**Keywords seem obvious/generic?**
- This is fine. Generic keywords still surface prospects
- More important to have relevant keywords than clever ones
- Combine generic + niche keywords for coverage

**How many keywords should I have?**
- AutoReach generates 10-20 per platform (good starting point)
- More keywords = higher volume but potentially lower quality
- Start with 10-15, monitor quality, adjust

**Can I edit AI-generated keywords?**
- Yes, absolutely. AutoReach suggestions are starting points
- Customize based on your market knowledge
- Add keywords you've validated in past campaigns

## Advanced Strategies

**Persona-Specific Keywords:**

Create multiple keyword sets for different personas:
- **Executive keywords:** "revenue growth", "board reporting", "strategic planning"
- **Operator keywords:** "process efficiency", "system integration", "team scaling"
- **Specialist keywords:** "data analysis", "financial modeling", "forecasting accuracy"

Run separate searches with persona-specific keywords, then combine results.

**Competitive Keyword Mining:**

- Identify prospects using competitor solutions
- Extract keywords they use discussing those solutions
- Create keywords targeting "switching from X to Y"
- Example: "leaving Salesforce" + your CRM name

**Seasonal Keyword Rotation:**

- Q1: Budget/planning-focused keywords ("budget cycle", "planning")
- Q2: Execution/implementation ("rolling out", "implementing")
- Q3: Review/optimization ("optimizing", "performance review")
- Q4: Strategic planning ("next year", "planning", "budget")

Match keyword themes to procurement seasonality in your market.
