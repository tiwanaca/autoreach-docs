# Approving Content

The Engagement Engine generates all content automatically, but you maintain full control through the **approval queue**. Review, edit, or reject any piece of content before it posts to your account.

## The Approval Queue

The Engagement Engine shows all pending content awaiting your review:

- **Pending Tweets**: AI-generated tweets ready to post
- **Pending Posts**: LinkedIn posts awaiting approval
- **Pending Engagement**: replies, likes, and other engagement actions
- **Auto-Replies**: responses to incoming engagement on your posts

Each item shows:
- The content text
- When it's scheduled to post
- What pillar/theme it aligns with
- Your approval options

## Reviewing Content

When you open the approval queue, you will see a feed of pending content:

{% hint style="info" %}
Take time reviewing the first week of content. This helps you calibrate the tone and ensure it matches how you'd write. After that, you can decide whether to manually review or enable auto-approval.
{% endhint %}

For each piece of content, you can:

1. **Approve**: post exactly as written
2. **Reject**: discard and generate a replacement
3. **Edit**: modify the text and then approve the edited version

## Approving Content

The simplest option: click **Approve** and the content posts as scheduled.

- No changes needed
- Content goes live at the scheduled time
- Move to the next pending item

## Rejecting Content

If something doesn't fit your voice or niche:

1. Click **Reject**
2. AutoReach generates a new alternative
3. Review the replacement in the queue
4. Approve or reject again

Rejected content is replaced immediately, so you always have fresh alternatives to review.

{% hint style="warning" %}
Rejecting content doesn't delay your posting schedule. AutoReach instantly generates a replacement to fill that time slot.
{% endhint %}

## Editing Before Approving

You can edit any piece of content:

1. Click **Edit** on the pending content
2. Modify the text as needed
3. Click **Save & Approve** to post your edited version
4. Or **Save & Review Later** to come back to it

Common edits:
- Adjusting tone or formality
- Adding specific examples or references
- Shortening or expanding the content
- Adding hashtags or mentions

The edited content posts at the scheduled time with your changes.

## Auto-Approval Mode

Once you're comfortable with the Engagement Engine's content quality, enable **auto-approval**:

- All pending content automatically posts on schedule
- No manual review needed
- Frees up your time
- You maintain the ability to view what posted in activity logs

To enable auto-approval:
1. Open Engagement Engine settings
2. Find "Content Approval" or "Auto-Approve"
3. Toggle **On**
4. Confirm you want to enable auto-posting

You can disable auto-approval anytime to return to manual review.

## When to Enable Auto-Approval

**Wait to enable auto-approval until:**
- You've reviewed at least 1 week of pending content
- You're comfortable with the tone and relevance
- You've edited a few pieces to calibrate the style
- You trust AutoReach's content quality

**Good candidates for auto-approval from day 1:**
- Teams with clear brand voice guidelines
- Accounts where consistency matters more than personalization
- Busy managers who want set-and-forget engagement

## Managing the Approval Queue

**Tips for staying on top of reviews:**

- **Review daily**: spend 5-10 minutes reviewing pending content each morning
- **Batch editing**: review all tweets, then all posts, then engagement
- **Use filters**: some dashboards let you filter by type (tweets, posts, replies)
- **Set reminders**: calendar reminder for daily approval review

**If you fall behind:**
- Pending content queues but doesn't post until approved
- No posts are lost. They wait for your review.
- Clear the queue during slower periods
- Consider enabling auto-approval temporarily if backlog builds up

## Content Quality Over Time

AutoReach's content generation improves as it learns:

- After 2 weeks, the system has learned your tone better
- After 4 weeks, generated content is highly personalized
- After 8 weeks, you'll likely see less need to edit

Early content may require more edits. This is normal. By week 3-4, approvals become quick checkmarks.

## Approval Workflow for Teams

If multiple people manage the account:

1. One person reviews pending content
2. Approves or edits
3. Post goes live as scheduled
4. Activity log shows who approved each piece

Assign one person as the "content approver" to maintain consistent tone.
